About Me

I am a passionate designer based in Vancouver, known for my unwavering dedication to pushing creative boundaries and delivering exceptional results. My journey in design has been a dynamic exploration of the intersection between architecture, game environment, and the art of digital illustration.

I firmly believe that inspiration comes from anywhere, and this belief permeates every facet of my work. From concept to execution, I pour my heart and soul into each project, ensuring it not only meets but exceeds the vision and expectations of my clients.

Achievements

Mental Health Awareness Campaign

As a graphic design major at BCIT, one of my most significant accomplishments was the design and execution of a campus-wide awareness campaign. Tasked with addressing a pressing issue on campus—mental health awareness—I conceptualized, designed, and implemented a visually compelling campaign. The campaign materials, including posters, flyers, and social media graphics, not only conveyed a message of support but also effectively raised awareness about available mental health resources. The campaign received widespread recognition, not only within the university community but also from mental health organizations, which commended its impact. This achievement not only showcased my design skills but also highlighted my ability to leverage design to address important social issues and connect with diverse audiences.

In my role, I successfully conceptualized, executed the design, collaborated with various stakeholders, crafted impactful messaging, earned recognition and awards, and left a lasting positive impact on the university community.

Campaign Conceptualization:

The achievement began with the conceptualization phase, where I researched the issue of mental health awareness on campus, identified key messaging points, and developed a comprehensive campaign strategy.

Design Execution:

Once the concept was solidified, I proceeded to create visually captivating designs that would effectively convey the campaign's message. This included designing posters, flyers, digital graphics, and social media content, ensuring consistency in branding and messaging across all materials.

Collaborative Effort:

To make the campaign successful, I collaborated closely with fellow students, faculty members, and mental health professionals. This teamwork allowed for a holistic approach, ensuring that the campaign resonated with a diverse audience and accurately represented the available mental health resources on campus.

Impactful Messaging:

One of the key strengths of the campaign was the thoughtful messaging. Each design element was carefully crafted to destigmatize mental health issues, promote a sense of community, and encourage individuals to seek help when needed. This approach contributed to the campaign's overall effectiveness.

Recognition and Awards:

The campaign received recognition and accolades not only within the university but also from mental health organizations. This external validation underscored the campaign's positive impact and demonstrated its significance in addressing mental health issues on campus.

Long-lasting Impact:

Beyond the immediate campaign launch, it left a lasting impact on the university community. It fostered an environment where discussions about mental health became more open, and students felt empowered to seek support and resources when facing mental health challenges.

Hands of Youth Rebranding Project

One of my proudest achievements during my graphic design studies at BCIT was my role as the lead designer for a local nonprofit's rebranding project. The organization, Hands of Youth, sought a fresh identity to better convey its mission and values. Collaborating closely with their team, I conducted extensive research to understand their core objectives and target audience. Leveraging this insight, I developed a comprehensive branding package, including a new logo, color scheme, and marketing materials. The rebranding initiative not only garnered unanimous praise from the nonprofit's board members but also resulted in a 30% increase in donor engagement and support within the first quarter of the launch. This achievement reinforced my belief in design's power to drive positive change and demonstrated my ability to translate a client's vision into a successful visual identity that resonates with both the organization and its supporters.
